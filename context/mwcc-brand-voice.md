# MWCC Brand Voice

**Read this when generating content, ad copy, emails, social posts, or landing pages for My World Childcare.**

For full brand context (centres, services, positioning), see `context/mwcc-brand-context.md`.

---

## Tone of voice

Warm. Professional. Reassuring. Parents are trusting us with the most important people in their lives — every word should feel like it comes from someone they can trust.

**Five tone descriptors:**
1. **Caring and nurturing** — not clinical or corporate
2. **Confident** — we know what we're doing; calm authority
3. **Approachable** — easy to read, feels like a knowledgeable friend
4. **Specific** — describe actual moments (breakfast time, story time, ratio in the Babies room) not generic claims
5. **Family-first** — every line answers "what does this mean for my child?"

---

## Voice rules — DO

- DO speak directly to parents — "you" + "your child"
- DO use specifics — "qualified early-childhood educators", "1:5 ratio in the Toddlers room", "CCS-approved"
- DO name the centre + suburb when relevant — "Armadale OSHC", "Seville Grove Babies room"
- DO mention CCS (Child Care Subsidy) early when discussing fees
- DO acknowledge parent reality — drop-off rush, work pressure, childcare cost
- DO end with a clear next step — "Book a tour", "Join the waitlist", "Call your nearest centre"

## Voice rules — DON'T

- DON'T say "best", "premier", "leading" — Australian Consumer Law claims must be defensible
- DON'T use "daycare" alone — "childcare" or "early learning" or specific service name (LDC, OSHC)
- DON'T use clinical or institutional language — "facility", "intake", "client"
- DON'T over-promise outcomes ("your child will be reading by 4")
- DON'T use children's photos without parental consent — always check before publishing
- DON'T name specific children or families in marketing copy
- DON'T use scarcity tactics that feel manipulative — childcare decisions are emotional
- DON'T use emojis in formal copy (landing pages, emails). Limited use OK on IG/FB social posts only.

---

## CTAs — preferred hierarchy

1. **Book a tour** (highest intent — closer to enrolment)
2. **Join the waitlist** (when at capacity)
3. **Get a personalised quote** (when fee questions dominate)
4. **Call us** (when parent prefers human contact)
5. **Download the centre info pack** (early-funnel)

Avoid "Learn more" and "Click here" — both too generic.

## Language do's

| Concept | Use this | Don't use |
|---|---|---|
| Childcare service | "childcare", "early learning" | "daycare" (informal/American) |
| Educators | "qualified early-childhood educators" | "staff", "carers" |
| Children | "your child", "the children" | "kids", "kiddos" |
| Subsidy | "CCS approved" / "Child Care Subsidy" | "government rebate" |
| Tour | "book a tour" | "visit our facility" |
| Centre | "centre" (Australian spelling) | "center" |
| Programme | "programme" or "program" (consistent within doc) | mix |

## Examples

### Good — IG caption

"Last Tuesday at Armadale Before-School Care, eight kids built a cardboard castle big enough to crawl through. Then they ate breakfast inside it.

This is what we mean by 'play-based learning' — open-ended materials, qualified educators who notice the moment, and time to follow a child's idea wherever it goes.

→ Book a tour at our Armadale OSHC: link in bio"

### Bad — IG caption

"🎉 BIG NEWS at MWCC Armadale!! Our amazing OSHC kids did such an awesome job today building castles!! Best childcare in Perth — book now before spots run out!!! 🏰✨"

(Reasons it's bad: emoji-heavy, claim of "best", scarcity tactic, capital BIG NEWS, no specific detail about what happened, no clear CTA.)

## Compliance + safety

- Always read `context/mwcc-business-config.json` for current centre rooms + services before claiming what we offer
- CCS rate claims must include "from" or "subject to eligibility" — fees vary
- NQS rating claims must match actual current rating (check OWNA or Quality Improvement Plan)
- Never name a competitor disparagingly
- Always include the privacy/photo-consent note when using imagery
