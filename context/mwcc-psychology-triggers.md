# Psychological Triggers Framework — My World Childcare

Related: `mwcc-brand-context.md` · `mwcc-marketing-strategy.md` · `MWCC-Knowledge-Base.md`

**Different from CB247:** Childcare buyer psychology is fundamentally different to gym buyer psychology. Parents are not buying a service for themselves — they're delegating care of the person they care most about. Every trigger below reflects that emotional core.

---

## Core Framework

Every piece of MWCC content must include at least **2 of these triggers** to maximise conversion. Compound use (3+) typically lifts response. Skip these and content reads generic.

### 1. TRUST (the #1 emotional gate for every parent)

Trust is the precondition. Without trust nothing else lands. Build it first.

- **Formula:** "Qualified people + real specifics + visible evidence"
- **Application:**
  - "Qualified early-childhood educators with current Working with Children Checks"
  - "Our Babies room runs 1:4 ratio — well above the WA mandated 1:5"
  - "Open since 2018 · approved provider · CCS approved across all 5 centres"
- **Avoid:** Vague trust language ("we care") that any centre could write. Trust comes from specific, defensible claims.

### 2. SOCIAL PROOF (other parents like me)

Parents trust other parents more than they trust marketing. Always include numbers, suburbs, real (anonymised) detail.

- **Formula:** "[X] families across [suburbs] + specific anonymised detail"
- **Application:**
  - "Over 400 families across Armadale, Midvale, Rockingham, Seville Grove, and Waikiki"
  - "12 families chose Seville Grove LDC this term — three came across from a national chain"
  - Direct parent text quote on graphic card (with consent): "We toured three centres. Midvale was the only one where the educators asked about my daughter's routine."
- **NEVER:** Use children's faces or names. Quotes from parents are text-only on branded graphic.

### 3. LOSS AVERSION (spots are finite — this is real for childcare)

Childcare is genuinely finite — waitlists fill, term enrolments lock, Vacation Care booking windows close. Lean on real scarcity, never manufactured.

- **Formula:** "[Specific window] closing + what happens after"
- **Application:**
  - "Vacation Care bookings close Friday 5pm. After that, families on the waitlist get first preference."
  - "Kindy 2027 places are confirmed in October. Tours booked after that are for the 2028 list."
  - "Last 3 spots in the Toddlers room at Waikiki — once full, families wait until the next room transition (typically mid-Q4)."
- **NEVER:** "Limited spots — book now!" unless Kelley has verified capacity that morning. False scarcity loses trust permanently.

### 4. FUTURE-ORIENTATION (child development → parent confidence)

Parents are buying their child's future. Connect today's care to tomorrow's outcome — without overclaiming.

- **Formula:** "Today at [centre], children explore [activity] — building [foundational skill]"
- **Application:**
  - "Mark-making in the Toddlers room today — early literacy foundation."
  - "Kindy children planted a vegetable garden this week — first lessons in patience, observation, and where food comes from."
  - "Play-based learning isn't downtime. It's how children practice the social and cognitive skills they'll use at school."
- **NEVER:** "Your child will be reading by 4" or any developmental guarantee. Compliance breach + parental psychology backlash if it doesn't happen.

### 5. IDENTITY (the "good working parent")

Frame MWCC as confirming the parent's positive self-image, not undermining it.

- **Formula:** "Parents who [admirable trait] choose MWCC"
- **Application:**
  - "For parents who refuse to choose between work and being present" (LDC angle)
  - "Working parents who know their child's day deserves the same intention as their own"
  - "Carefully chosen by 400+ Perth families who did the research"
- **NEVER:** Shame the parent. Childcare buyers don't tolerate shame triggers — they're already navigating massive guilt. Shame loses sales here, even though it works in fitness marketing.

### 6. AUTHORITY (regulatory + qualifications signal)

Establish credibility through verifiable regulatory + professional credentials.

- **Formula:** "[Specific qualification / regulation] + what it means for the child"
- **Application:**
  - "Educators hold a Certificate III, Diploma, or higher in Early Childhood Education"
  - "ACECQA approved provider · WA DGE licensed across all 5 centres"
  - "Each centre meets the National Quality Standard across all 7 quality areas" (only if currently true and verified per `state/mwcc-nqs-ratings.json`)
- **NEVER:** Cite a specific NQS rating tier unless verified current. Authority claims must survive a regulator's audit.

### 7. RECIPROCITY (give value before asking)

Parents respond to centres that help them think clearly — before pitching the enrolment.

- **Formula:** "Free [resource] that solves a real parent problem"
- **Application:**
  - "Free 5-minute CCS calculator — see what your weekly cost would actually be"
  - "Download the school holiday planner: 14-day activity ideas split by age (free, no email required)"
  - "Book a tour at any centre and receive our Settling In Guide — a 7-page handbook from our educators on how to make the first 30 days easier"
- **NEVER:** Lock the value behind an email form before the parent has trust. Use lead-gen forms only AFTER a parent has touched a high-trust page (tour widget, CCS calculator).

### 8. SPECIFICITY (anti-generic — defeats every "stock" childcare ad)

Almost every competitor in Perth posts generic claims ("nurturing environment", "play-based learning"). Specificity is MWCC's structural advantage.

- **Formula:** "Real moment + real time + real place + real detail"
- **Application:**
  - "Tuesday morning in the Toddlers room at Seville Grove — children had ten minutes to use the small magnifying glasses on a single autumn leaf."
  - "The Kindy room at Midvale starts with a 15-minute group provocation — today it was 'where does rain come from?'"
  - "Last week the Before School Care group at Armadale debated the rules for a chess club they started"
- **NEVER:** "Every day is special at MWCC!" — it could be any centre. Defeats the trigger by removing specifics.

### 9. PEER DECISIONING (childcare decisions get socialised)

Parents talk to other parents. Build content that gives parents words to repeat — "I went with My World because [reason]."

- **Formula:** "One concrete reason that's easy to remember + easy to repeat"
- **Application:**
  - "We chose My World because the centre director knew our daughter's name by tour day 2."
  - "We went with Midvale because the educator team have all been there 3+ years — staff continuity matters at this age."
  - "We picked Waikiki because the centre tour included 30 minutes with the head educator, not a salesperson."
- **NEVER:** Use generic reasons ("great atmosphere"). Useless when repeated.

## Per-channel emphasis

Not every channel suits every trigger. Lean as follows:

| Channel | Strong triggers | Weak triggers |
|---|---|---|
| **Meta Ads (FB/IG paid)** | Trust + Social Proof + Loss Aversion | Reciprocity (works but burns budget) |
| **Google Search Ads** | Authority + Specificity + Trust | Identity (parents searching want facts, not framing) |
| **IG organic feed** | Specificity + Future-orientation + Peer Decisioning | Loss aversion (looks pushy outside paid context) |
| **GBP posts** | Trust + Authority + Specificity | Identity (too personal for a GBP post) |
| **Email (broadcast)** | Reciprocity + Loss Aversion (real scarcity) + Social Proof | Identity (works in onboarding emails, not broadcast) |
| **Landing pages** | All 9 — landing pages are the only place to layer multiple triggers in one flow | — |
| **SEO blogs** | Reciprocity + Authority + Future-orientation | Loss Aversion (off-tone in informational content) |

## Objection Removal

Every piece of mid-funnel content should address at least one of these common parent barriers:

| Objection | Reframe |
|---|---|
| **"Too expensive"** | "CCS reduces your weekly cost significantly. A family earning $100K-$150K typically gets 50-65% subsidised. Use our free CCS calculator to see your actual net cost." |
| **"What if my child doesn't settle"** | "Our 7-day settling plan is built around your child. The Babies room runs at 1:4 — every child gets a primary educator who knows their routine. If after 2 weeks settling isn't working, we adjust the plan or refund unused fees." |
| **"Long waitlist"** | "Book a tour now to be assessed for upcoming room transitions — most enrolments come from families who tour 3-6 months ahead, not last-minute." |
| **"Strangers caring for my child"** | "Meet the centre director and your child's primary educator at the tour. We schedule tours when educators are present, not just at quiet times. The first 30 minutes are with the team your child would actually be with." |
| **"Logistics — work + childcare hours"** | "Our LDC centres open 6:30am - 6:30pm. OSHC runs Before School (6:30am-8:30am) and After School (3:00pm-6:30pm). Most working parents fit either side of a standard day. Flexible booking days available." |
| **"Worried about NQS rating / quality"** | "Our quality area details are public on the ACECQA register. Tour the centre — quality is visible in 10 minutes of watching how educators interact with children." |
| **"What about siblings at different ages?"** | "Midvale and Seville Grove are full-spectrum — Babies through Kindy through OSHC. Siblings at different ages can be at the same centre with familiar educators." |

## What CB247 triggers DON'T transfer to MWCC

For reference — these gym triggers must NOT be used in MWCC content:

| CB247 trigger | Why it fails for MWCC |
|---|---|
| "Still putting everyone else first?" (shame → identity) | Parents are already overloaded with guilt. Shame triggers backfire badly. |
| "Less than one coffee a day" (price reframe) | Trivialises a major monthly expense. Parents take fee decisions seriously. |
| "Gymtimidation removal" | Different problem space. Childcare parents fear quality + safety, not social judgment. |
| "FIFO friendly" | FIFO parent ICP doesn't map. Childcare clients prioritise consistency over flexibility. |
| "First visit free" | "Free trial day" is not standard for childcare (children need orientation, not trial). |
