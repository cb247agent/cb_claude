Most Perth gyms are built around a simple assumption: you live nearby and train a few times a week, every week. That assumption falls apart the moment you go FIFO.

What the Research Says About FIFO Health

This isn't just an inconvenience — the data shows FIFO life is genuinely hard on the body and mind, which makes consistent training matter more, not less.

The WA Mental Health Commission's study of 3,000 FIFO workers found 33% experience high or very high psychological distress — compared with 17% of other workers and around 10% of average Australians.[1] A separate WA study found more than 80% of FIFO workers were at increased risk of type 2 diabetes or heart disease, and that workers exercise less and sleep worse on-shift than off.[2]

Exercise is one of the most effective tools against exactly those risks. A 2022 review in the British Journal of Sports Medicine found that just 30–60 minutes of strength training a week is associated with around a 15% lower risk of early death and ~17% lower cardiovascular risk.[3] The catch? You can only get that benefit from a gym you can actually use around your roster.

Recovery on the Swing — Backed by Science

Those cold-plunge videos are half right — and recovery genuinely matters when you're fighting camp fatigue and broken sleep.

  • Sauna is the real deal. The landmark Finnish KIHD study (JAMA Internal Medicine, 2015) found people using a sauna 4–7 times a week had a 40% lower risk of all-cause mortality and roughly half the fatal cardiovascular risk of once-a-week users.[5]
  • Ice baths — mind the timing. Cold water immersion reduces soreness, but research in the Journal of Physiology shows plunging straight after a heavy lifting session can blunt muscle growth.[6] Use it on rest days or after conditioning — not right after you lift.
  • The "cortisol" trend is mostly noise. Cortisol is a normal hormone your body needs; genuinely high levels (Cushing's) affect ~0.005% of people. Consistent training, sleep and recovery beat any "cortisol detox."[7]
