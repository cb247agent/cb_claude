## 1. Executive Summary: The "Facility Advantage" Gap
Current research confirms ChasingBetter247 (CB247) holds a unique "Middle Ground" position in Perth's northern corridor (Malaga/Ellenbrook). While competitors are locked in a price war or niche specialization, CB247 is the only operator providing premium recovery and childcare at a budget-parity price point.

### Top 3 Market Threats
*   **Revo Fitness (The Price Leader):** Aggressively undercutting on base price ($9.69) and capturing the 24/7 Reformer Pilates market with their Level 2 ($12.69) tier. They are the #1 threat to our Ellenbrook and Malaga membership volume.
*   **Ryderwear Gym Malaga (The Community Leader):** Owns the "serious lifter" and "athlete" aesthetic. Their brand presence on social media creates high organic loyalty that is harder to break with price alone.
*   **Anytime Fitness/Snap Fitness (The Convenience Leaders):** Maintain market share through sheer volume of locations and national brand recognition, despite higher weekly rates (~$15-18).

## 4. Marketing & Ad Strategy Analysis
*   **Revo Fitness:** Focusing on "The Studio" 24/7 Reformer Pilates. Their ads are high-production, minimalistic, and price-forward. They are positioning themselves as the "premium budget" choice.
*   **Ryderwear:** Creative leans heavily into "vibe," lighting, and community. Their marketing is lifestyle-first, gym-second.

## 6. Revo Fitness: Deep Dive (#1 Threat)
Revo's transition to a two-tier model ($9.69 Level 1 / $12.69 Level 2) is a direct attempt to capture the boutique fitness market (Pilates/Yoga) at a mass-market price.
*   **Vulnerability:** Their "Level 2" is now **more expensive** ($12.69) than CB247's "All-in" price ($11.95).
*   **Counter-Attack:** Our messaging should pivot to: *"Why pay more for just Pilates? Get Pilates + Sauna + Ice Bath + Kids Hub for $11.95."*
