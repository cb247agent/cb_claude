## Executive Summary

Revo Fitness is CB247's most dangerous price competitor in Perth's north corridor. Their Malaga and Ellenbrook clubs undercut CB247 on base price ($9.69–$12.69/wk vs CB247's $11.95/wk) and have aggressive 24/7 Reformer Pilates ("The Studio"). However, Revo has zero answer for CB247's recovery suite (sauna + ice bath) and Kids Hub — the two features that make CB247 a genuinely complete gym for FIFO workers and young families.

**Bottom line:** Revo wins on price and Pilates. CB247 wins on recovery, family, and FIFO-specific value. The comparison page should lean hard into recovery + family + FIFO freeze as the differentiator.

## Strategic Observations

### Malaga Market
Revo Malaga is predominantly a **"lifter factory"** — strong free weights culture, bodybuilders, powerlifters. It lacks the lifestyle and recovery positioning that CB247 has. For FIFO workers who want to train AND recover properly, CB247 is the only option that delivers both.

**Key insight:** A FIFO worker on swing can train at Revo for $9.69/week — but they can't recover in a sauna/ice bath, and if they have kids, there's no childcare. CB247's total package is stronger for the actual ICP.

### Ellenbrook Market
Revo Ellenbrook attracts young professionals and singles. CB247's **Kids Hub** is the single biggest differentiator in Ellenbrook — Revo cannot replicate this without major renovation and staffing investment. Parents searching for "gym with kids ellenbrook" have no organic path to Revo.

### SEO Positioning
Revo dominates "gym malaga" and "gym ellenbrook" through high-authority domain authority. CB247 cannot out-rank Revo on raw domain authority quickly — but can win on **keyword clusters Revo can't own:**
- "sauna malaga" — Revo has no sauna
- "ice bath malaga" — Revo has no ice bath
- "kids gym malaga" — Revo has no kids hub
- "bath house malaga" — Revo has no bath house
- "fifo gym perth" — Revo has generic freeze, no FIFO-specific messaging
