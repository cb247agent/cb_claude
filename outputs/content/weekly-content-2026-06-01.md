#### What is Reformer Pilates?

Reformer Pilates is a full-body workout using a reformer machine — a sliding platform with springs, straps, and a footbar. The springs provide resistance, building strength while keeping movements low-impact and controlled.

**Benefits:**
- Builds core strength and stability
- Improves flexibility and posture
- Low-impact — safe for joints and injury recovery
- Full-body workout — targets every muscle group
- Suitable for all fitness levels (beginner to advanced)

Whether you're recovering from an injury, training for a sport, or looking to build lean muscle, Reformer Pilates delivers results without the high-impact stress of traditional gym workouts.

#### Reformer Pilates vs Mat Pilates — What's the Difference?

| Feature | Reformer Pilates | Mat Pilates |
|---------|------------------|-------------|
| Equipment | Reformer machine with springs and resistance | Bodyweight only (sometimes with small props) |
| Resistance | Adjustable spring resistance | Bodyweight resistance |
| Difficulty | Easier for beginners (machine guides movement) | Harder for beginners (requires more core control) |
| Intensity | Can be scaled from rehab to elite athlete | Limited by bodyweight resistance |
| Best For | Building strength + flexibility, injury recovery | Core control, home workouts |

**Verdict:** Reformer Pilates offers more variety, more resistance, and faster results — especially for beginners and people recovering from injury.

**Q: What should I wear?**  
A: Fitted activewear (leggings + tank top or fitted shirt). Grippy socks are recommended for better stability on the reformer carriage.

**Q: How often should I do Reformer Pilates?**

**Q: Can I do Reformer Pilates if I'm injured?**
