# "Unsure" Objection Playbook — CB247 Save Calls

**Audience:** Reception team (Malaga + Ellenbrook), briefed by Angela
**Active from:** 12 June 2026 (current top cancel reason in Cleverwaiver: "Unsure" — 47% of submitted responses)
**Pool size:** ~50 members per week citing "Unsure" at exit
**Target save rate:** 35–45% (this objection is the softest in the funnel — they haven't decided, they're testing us)
**PGM tracking tag:** `SAVE-UNSURE-{date}` (apply to every outcome — see §7)

---

## The Hook (memorise this single line)

> **"Before you decide — let's pause it instead. Four weeks frozen, no charges, your spot stays. You'll know by then."**

That's the whole pitch. The member said "Unsure" — they're not telling us no, they're telling us they haven't said yes yet. The job isn't to convince them to stay, it's to **buy time before they leave**.

**What this offer costs the club:** $0 marginal cost. Freeze is free — we already offer it standard for FIFO. We're using it as a decision-delay tool, not a discount.

**Eligibility:**
- Active member who selected "Unsure" on Cleverwaiver exit survey
- Has NOT yet cited a specific reason (price / time / facility / staff complaint) — those route to other playbooks
- Agrees to **at least 4 weeks frozen** (minimum freeze window per CB247 policy)
- Can be combined with a Recovery add-on trial if they go through with reactivation — never volunteer this on the first call

---

## 1. Opening (every call, every time)

```
"Hi [Name], it's [Your Name] from CB247.
 I saw your cancellation come through this morning and noticed you put 'Unsure'
 in the exit reason. Wanted to ring you personally before it processes —
 you got two minutes?"
```

**Do:** stay warm, use their first name twice in the first 30 seconds, mention you saw the specific reason (it shows they were heard).

**Don't:** open with "Why are you cancelling?" (defensive) or "We hate to see you go" (passive-aggressive). The opener has to make them want to keep talking, not feel cornered.

If they say "not a good time" → **"Totally — when's better today? I'm not here for a long pitch, just a quick chat."** Lock the callback in PGM with a specific time. No vague "later this week."

---

## 2. Diagnose — what does "Unsure" actually mean?

The Cleverwaiver "Unsure" answer hides four real reasons. Your second question identifies which one:

> **"Fair enough. When you said 'unsure' — was it the money, the time you've got, the gym itself, or something at home?"**

That open-ended four-option question gets them to pick the real driver without you guessing wrong. Listen for which they answer first.

| What they say | What it actually means | Route to |
|---|---|---|
| "It's the money" / "$11.95 adds up" | Price-conscious, not committed | §3a — Freeze + Don't Quit Winter offer |
| "I'm not coming in" / "I don't have time" | Habit broken, but not against us | §3b — Habit reset offer |
| "I haven't been using it enough" | Same as above + guilt | §3b — Habit reset offer |
| "I just need a break" / "Burnt out" | High recovery potential — soft objection | §3c — Pure freeze, no add-on |
| Vague / multiple / changes mid-sentence | Genuinely undecided | §3c — Pure freeze, no add-on |

**Critical:** if they cite a *specific complaint* (staff name, facility issue, broken equipment) — STOP this script. Escalate to Angela. That's a Tier-2 save call and needs different handling.

---

## 3. The Concession Menu (pick ONE — never offer all three)

### 3a. Price-driven Unsure → Pause + Don't Quit Winter

```
"Got it. Here's what I can do — pause your membership for 4 weeks, no
 charges during that time, and if you reactivate within 60 days we'll
 throw in free Sauna + Ice Bath access until 31 August. That's our
 Don't Quit Winter offer. Want me to set that up?"
```

This is the **Don't Quit Winter** offer, cross-referenced from that playbook. Don't redo their work — read `outputs/playbooks/dont-quit-winter-save-call.md` for the full mechanics.

### 3b. Habit-broken Unsure → Pause + Class Reset

```
"That happens to most members — the routine breaks and it's hard to come
 back. Here's what we'll do: I'll pause your membership for 4 weeks while
 you sort the calendar out, and when you come back I'll personally book
 you into your first three classes. No charge for the pause, no pressure
 to renew. Sound good?"
```

The hook here isn't the freeze — it's the **personal class booking when they return**. Removes the "where do I even start" friction that broke the habit. Note the member's preferred class type in PGM so Reception can do the booking when they reactivate.

### 3c. Genuine "I don't know" → Pause, no extras

```
"Honestly? Easiest thing — let's pause it for 4 weeks. Nothing happens,
 nothing charges, your account stays exactly as it is. If 4 weeks from
 now you're sure you want to leave, you ring me back and I'll process it
 same day. If you're not sure or you want to come back, you log in and
 unfreeze. Fair?"
```

For pure undecided members, the **lowest-friction offer wins**. Don't try to sweeten it — adding an add-on offer here implies you're trying to "win" them, which triggers the buyer's remorse loop. Just give them the door back.

---

## 4. The Close (after they say yes)

```
"Perfect. I'm setting it up now — your membership will be paused from
 [today] and you'll reactivate automatically on [today + 4 weeks].
 I'll send you a confirmation text in the next 5 minutes. Anything I
 should know about that day in case I need to reach you?"
```

**Do:** confirm the exact pause-end date in their words. Send the confirmation text WHILE on the call — they hear the ding. That ding is the deposit on trust.

**Don't:** thank them for "staying" — they didn't, they paused. Don't talk about the next step ("see you in 4 weeks!") — that's pressure. End on the confirmation, not the future.

---

## 5. The "No-Save" Exit (when they decline the freeze)

```
"All good — I'll process the cancellation now. One last thing: if you
 ever want to come back, you've got 90 days to reactivate at the same
 rate. After that it's whatever the current pricing is — which is going
 up, just so you know. Want me to flag your file so Reception knows
 you're an option-to-return?"
```

**Do:** process the cancel calmly and professionally. The "90-day option-to-return" line is a low-stakes door that ~20% of cancels walk back through within 60 days.

**Don't:** apologise for the call. Don't volunteer a discount you didn't offer earlier (it tells them they could have negotiated harder). Don't extend the conversation past the cancel confirmation.

---

## 6. Compliance — read before every shift

- **Never** name another gym (Revo / Anytime / Snap / Ryderwear) even if the member volunteers it. *"I get it — every gym has its strengths. What's working for you matters more than what they're doing."*
- **Never** claim CB247 facilities will heal / cure / treat / burn fat. *Use:* "support your recovery routine," "help you stay consistent," "build your weekly habit."
- **Never** say "we're the only gym with sauna" — Ryderwear has sauna + Reformer Pilates too. Stick to your actual differentiators.
- **Never** imply Recovery / Reformer Pilates / ChasingRX are included in the base $11.95/wk. They are paid add-ons. The Don't Quit Winter offer covers Sauna + Ice Bath specifically because that's the call.

**True CB247 differentiators (use these confidently):** Kids Hub · 24/7 access · Traditional Sauna + Ice Bath · FIFO-friendly freeze (no other Perth chain matches). Mention 1–2, never all 4 in the same call — that sounds like a pitch.
