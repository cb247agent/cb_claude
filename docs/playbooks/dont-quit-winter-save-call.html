Don't Quit Winter — Save-Call Playbook

Rendered 2026-06-12 · source: outputs/playbooks/dont-quit-winter-save-call.md

Audience: Brand Manager + Reception team (Malaga + Ellenbrook) Active period: 1 June – 31 August 2026 (Winter campaign window) Pool size at launch: 352 future-cancel contracts (279 unique members) Target save rate: 30% (≈ 84 saves preserving ~$4,800/mo recurring revenue at zero CAC) PGM tracking tag: SAVE-DONTQUIT-2026 (apply to every save outcome, see §6)

The Offer (memorise this single line)

"Freeze instead of cancel — we'll cover your Recovery month while you're paused. Free Sauna + Ice Bath through 31 August."

That's it. Don't add layers, don't apologise for the pause minimums, don't volunteer discounts that aren't in this doc.

Eligibility:

  • Active member in the future-cancel pipeline (already submitted cancellation, contract end date in the future)
  • Agrees to freeze for at least 4 weeks instead of completing the cancellation
  • One offer per member · doesn't stack with Winter Is Coming acquisition pricing

What the offer costs the club: $0 marginal cost — Recovery facility is sunk. We're trading the perceived value of the add-on (normally $20/wk) for a 4-week pause window that buys us time to re-engage the member.

1. Opening (every call, every time)

"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from CB247 — I saw your cancellation come through and I wanted to call you personally before it goes through. Got two minutes?"

Do: stay warm, never read off a script verbatim, use their first name twice in the first 30 seconds.

Don't: open with "I see you've cancelled" (passive, accusatory) or "Why are you cancelling?" (defensive). The first sentence makes them want to keep talking — or it doesn't.

If they say "not a good time" → "No worries — when's a better time today? I just want a quick chat, not a long pitch." Lock in a callback time. Log it.

2. Diagnose the real reason (don't guess from the survey)

The CleverWaiver survey gives you a clue but it's not the whole story. The real reason usually comes out in the second answer, not the first.

"I respect your decision — totally fine if you want to go ahead. Before I process it, what changed since you joined? What's not working anymore?"

Listen for the deeper "because":

  • "Not using it enough" → because what? Schedule? Motivation? Wrong session times?
  • "Switched to another gym" → because what? Cheaper? Closer? Friend joined elsewhere?
  • "Money's tight" → because what? Temporary (mortgage rate change) or permanent (job loss)?

Take ~30 seconds to listen. Don't interrupt to pitch. The freeze offer lands harder after they've explained themselves than before.

3. Three Objection Branches (top reasons from the exit survey)

The current data shows three actionable reasons drive most cancellations. Pick the branch that matches what they actually said — don't force the offer where it doesn't fit.

Branch A — "Not using enough" (≈30% of actionable cancellations)

What they're really saying: "I'm paying for something I'm not using, so I feel stupid every time I see the direct debit."

Pivot: Don't argue. Acknowledge first, then pivot to the freeze.

"That's fair — and honestly, that's the reason we built the freeze option. Instead of cancelling and starting over later (paying a join fee, getting a new contract), freeze it for a month. No debits. And we'll throw in the Recovery month free when you come back — that means free sauna and ice bath sessions for the first 4 weeks. People who use Recovery come in 2× more often. Want me to switch you to freeze instead?"

Why this works: Freezing eliminates the "wasting money" feeling immediately. The free Recovery month gives them a reason to come back, not just an absence of guilt.

Soft close: "Even if you don't end up coming back, no harm done — we don't reactivate without your say-so. Want me to do it now?"

Branch B — "Switched to another gym" (≈25% of actionable cancellations)

What they're really saying: Two sub-flavours — listen for which one:

  • (B1) Price-driven: "Anytime/Revo is $7/wk" → they're chasing the dollar
  • (B2) Friend/community-driven: "My mate joined [X]" → social pull

Pivot for B1 (price):

"I get it — we're not the cheapest, and we never will be. But before you go, two things: one, we just dropped winter pricing to $11.95/wk if you'd consider re-joining as a new member after winter — same price as Anytime, with all of our facilities. Two, while you're deciding, freeze for a month with free Recovery. That gives you 4 weeks to compare side-by-side, without paying either of us. Worst case, you cancel in 4 weeks instead of today. Sound fair?"

Why this works: You're not arguing the price — you're giving them a 4-week trial of staying. The Recovery they don't have at Anytime/Revo is the comparison their gut will make.

Pivot for B2 (community pull):

"That's actually one of the best reasons I've heard. Quick thing — if your mate isn't getting Recovery there, you could pause us for a month, do free Recovery sessions here, and still train with them at the other place. A lot of people end up doing both. Want me to set you up on freeze so you've got it as an option?"

Why this works: You don't fight the friend pull. You make staying with CB247 a complement, not a competitor.

Branch C — "Financial pressure" (≈15% of actionable cancellations)

What they're really saying: Real money problem (job, health, rent). Don't pitch — listen first.

Acknowledge with care:

"That's genuinely tough — I appreciate you telling me. Before we process the cancellation, I'd rather pause it than have you start over later when money frees up. Freeze for a month, no debits, and when you're ready to come back we'll cover the Recovery for a month. That way it's there for you, just not running. No pressure either way."

Important — what NOT to offer:

  • ❌ Do not offer a permanent discount. We are not negotiating the rate. Discounts erode price integrity across the base.
  • ❌ Do not offer free months beyond the Recovery add-on. The free-month track is for acquisition, not retention.
  • ❌ Do not promise to waive future fees.

If they push for a discount: "I can't drop the rate — but the freeze costs you nothing this month and we keep your spot at the current rate when you come back. Let me set that up."

Escalation: If member is in genuine hardship (visible distress, health issue, redundancy) → process the cancellation and offer free 4-week re-engagement when they message back. Log the date. Flag to Brand Manager for personal follow-up at the 8-week mark.

4. The Freeze-vs-Cancel Pivot (the close)

When you've identified the reason and offered the matching branch, close with the freeze framing, never the "stay" framing:

"Cool — to lock that in, I'll switch you from cancellation to freeze. 4-week minimum freeze, no charges that period, free Recovery the moment you come off freeze. Confirm I'm OK to do that?"

Make it sound administrative, not emotional. People will say yes to a paperwork change faster than to a commitment.

Get a verbal yes. Then process immediately while they're on the call.

5. Save Outcomes (4 categories — log every call in PGM)

After every save call, log one of these four outcomes. The Brand Manager reviews these weekly.

OutcomeWhat it meansNext step
SAVED-FREEZESwitched to freeze, accepted Recovery offerCalendar 28-day check-in. Re-engage with class rec when they unfreeze.
SAVED-RETAINEDStayed without freeze (e.g. just needed reassurance)Tag profile · weekly engagement check for 4 weeks.
LOST-CANCEL-CONFIRMEDConfirmed cancellation, no saveProcess. Add to 90-day win-back pool.
LOST-NO-CONTACTCouldn't reach after 2 attemptsProcess at contract end. Add to win-back pool.

Two contact attempts = call + SMS within 5 business days of cancellation submission. After that, no more calls — respect their decision.

6. Tracking — PGM Tag Convention

Every save outcome gets two PGM tags so we can track save-rate end-to-end:

  1. Outcome tag (one of):
  • SAVE-DONTQUIT-2026-FREEZE
  • SAVE-DONTQUIT-2026-RETAINED
  • SAVE-DONTQUIT-2026-LOST-CONFIRMED
  • SAVE-DONTQUIT-2026-LOST-NOCONTACT
  1. Reason tag (one of):
  • REASON-NOT-USING
  • REASON-SWITCHED-GYM
  • REASON-FINANCIAL
  • REASON-OTHER (specify in notes)

These tags flow into promo_tracking.dont-quit-winter-2026 in state/membership-data.json and surface on the Membership page as the Save Rate KPI.

8. Daily Cadence (during 4-week launch window)

  • Monday 09:00 — Brand Manager pulls fresh future-cancel list from PGM. Hands lists to reception (Malaga + Ellenbrook).
  • Mon–Fri 11:00–14:00 — Reception runs save calls. 2 calls every 15 minutes during quiet front-desk windows.
  • Daily EOD — Reception logs outcomes in PGM with both tags from §6.
  • Friday 16:00 — Brand Manager runs save-rate report (manual export until automated). Posts headline in team channel: "Week N: X reached, Y saved, Z rate."
  • Monday meeting — Performance Review page auto-promotes save-call action card at 14 days (Wave 6). Verdict it with the actual rate. Feeds back into next month's Promo Concept Generator (Wave 5).
