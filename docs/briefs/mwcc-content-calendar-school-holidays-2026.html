MWCC Content Calendar — School Holidays Window 2026

Period covered: Mon 8 Jun → Fri 31 Jul 2026 (8 weeks) Strategic anchor: WA Term 2 school holidays — Sat 4 Jul → Sun 17 Jul 2026 Author: AI (brand-aware skill) · Active business: MWCC · Generated: 7 Jun 2026 Owners: Jordan (content/creative) · Joanne (scheduling/paid) · Kelley (GBP + reviews) · John (SEO web)

1. Why school holidays are the most important window

Three demand spikes converge in the same 4-week period — and the same content can serve all three.

Audience What they need MWCC service Revenue lever
ICP 4 — Vacation Care families School holiday cover for 5-12 yr-olds OSHC Vacation Care (Armadale · Midvale · Rockingham · Seville Grove) Direct booking revenue, full programme fills
ICP 2 — OSHC term 3 families Before/After School Care from Mon 20 Jul OSHC term 3 enrolment Recurring fee revenue, occupancy lift
ICP 1 + ICP 3 — LDC researching parents Parents pause work over holidays → research childcare for term 3 + 2027 kindy LDC enrolment (Midvale · Seville Grove · Waikiki) Tour bookings → enrolment pipeline → occupancy lift

Why ads visibility matters now: Parents start searching ~3 weeks before the holiday window. Organic content + GBP posts + paid ads must run in concert — organic builds trust, paid captures the click. If we go dark organically while running ads, conversion drops because the brand looks transactional.

Network occupancy goal: lift from ~51% → 56% by 31 Jul (5-pt lift). Each percentage point ≈ 4-5 new enrolments network-wide.

3.5 VISUAL CONTENT RULES — locked 7 Jun 2026

NO photos of children. Period. This policy supersedes every visual recipe in this calendar. If a recipe below implies a photo of a child (or could be interpreted to), default to one of the approved visual options:

Approved visual Use for Notes
Educators (with written consent) "Centre life" posts · educator spotlights · team photos · tour preview Get consent in writing, store in mwcc-team-roster.md notes
Centre spaces & rooms (empty OR with educators) Behind-the-scenes · room transitions · winter setups · "what a tour looks like" Tidy beforehand. Caption tells the story of what happens here.
Materials & children's artwork (no faces, no names) Activity highlights · play-based learning examples · craft outputs A finished painting, the cardboard castle, the science-experiment setup. Description in caption — NOT child's name.
Branded graphics (lavender + purple palette) Programme announcements · CCS info · tour CTA cards · stat callouts Jordan's design templates. Always-safe option.
Parent text quotes on graphics (with parent consent) Testimonial posts · trust-building No parent face required — quote card design only.
Storytelling captions Anywhere — captions can describe what children did without showing them "Last Tuesday at Armadale Before-School Care, eight kids built a cardboard castle…" — fine. Image accompanying = the cardboard castle, NOT the kids.

What this rules out: Reels of children playing. Carousel slides of children in rooms. "Day in the life" stories that show children. Group shots. Any image where a child's face, body, or identifying feature is visible.

Why this is locked: Compliance + privacy. No image release process = no published images. This is non-negotiable.

If you (Jordan, AI, anyone) cannot make a recipe below work without showing a child → re-cast it using one of the approved visuals above, or drop the post entirely.
