School holiday survival guide for Perth working parents

Updated June 2026 · The practical plan for the four school breaks each year — Vacation Care, activities, family time, and how to avoid running out of leave by August.

For Perth working parents, the school calendar is a quietly stressful document. Twelve weeks of school holidays a year. Eight weeks of annual leave (if you're lucky). And one set of children who, for some reason, want to do something fun every day.

This guide is for parents in that gap. It walks through how Vacation Care actually works in WA, what a realistic holiday plan looks like, and how to spread your own annual leave so the second half of the year isn't a logistical scramble. We'll keep the activity lists short — this is mostly about the planning.

The Perth school holiday calendar — at a glance

WA public schools run on a four-term year, with breaks between each term and a longer break over summer:

BreakApprox. lengthTypical timing
Term 1 → Term 2 (Autumn)2 weeksMid-April
Term 2 → Term 3 (Winter)2 weeksEarly-mid July
Term 3 → Term 4 (Spring)2 weeksLate September / early October
Term 4 → Term 1 (Summer)~6 weeksMid-December to late January

The summer break is the one that catches working parents out — six weeks is longer than most families can cover with leave alone. The shorter breaks add up too: 2 + 2 + 2 weeks = a further six weeks across the year. Total: 12 weeks of school holidays vs. typically 4 weeks of annual leave.

Independent and Catholic schools sometimes run slightly different dates. Check your school's specific calendar each January — pupil-free days and early-finish days vary.

What is Vacation Care, and how does it fit?

Vacation Care is full-day care for school-age children during school holidays, run under the Outside School Hours Care (OSHC) umbrella. It's the most common option Perth working parents use to cover the holiday gap.

A typical Vacation Care day runs from around 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM. The programme is themed by week (Science Week, Sports Week, Movie Week — varies by centre), and includes a mix of on-site activities, incursions (visiting performers, animals, workshops) and excursions (zoo, bowling, beach trips, museums).

Vacation Care is approved under the Child Care Subsidy framework, so eligible families pay subsidised fees calculated the same way as before-and-after-school care. The CCS hourly rate cap for OSHC + Vacation Care in 2025-26 is $9.93 per hour.

Book early. Perth Vacation Care places fill quickly — especially for the summer break and the first week of each shorter holiday. Many centres open bookings 4-6 weeks ahead. If you wait until the last week of term, you'll be on a waitlist.

The realistic holiday plan: 3 categories

Most working parents we talk to spread their 12 weeks of school holidays across three categories. None of them are "right" — you mix them based on your leave balance, your child's age, and your budget.

1. Vacation Care (the backbone)

For working parents, Vacation Care typically covers 50-70% of school holiday days. It's the most reliable option — booked in advance, structured day, social for the child, and CCS-supported on cost. Many families use Vacation Care 3-4 days per week and keep one or two days for family activities.

2. Annual leave (used strategically)

The trap is using your annual leave on the closest break. Instead, plan leave for:

  • The first week of summer break (the household needs a reset after Term 4)
  • One week mid-summer when boredom peaks
  • One week somewhere mid-year for a proper family trip
  • The last week before Term 4 if you can — it's a long stretch from July to October

3. Family help, swaps and friend playdates

The unofficial third category. Grandparents on Tuesdays. A friend's house on a Wednesday in exchange for hosting their child the following Wednesday. A holiday club at the local sports centre for a morning. None of these need to cover full weeks — they just fill the gaps.

What goes wrong (and how to plan around it)

A few recurring patterns we see Perth parents fall into — and how to dodge them.

The "I'll book Vacation Care next week" trap

The most popular Vacation Care weeks (Movie Week, Excursion Week, the first week of summer) fill 4-6 weeks ahead. Mid-year — when you're deep in Term 2 and exhausted — is exactly when you should be booking July's Vacation Care. If you can, do all your bookings for the year in February.

The "we'll just do family stuff" optimism

This works for short breaks (2 weeks) if you have flexible work. It rarely works for the 6-week summer break. By week 3, the structure-of-the-day question becomes hard, and "what are we doing today" becomes a daily negotiation. Mix in 2-3 Vacation Care days per week to give your child some social variety and yourself some quiet hours.

The "no plan for pupil-free days" surprise

Schools take 4-6 pupil-free days per year on top of the school holidays. These are usually for staff PD or end-of-term admin. Most OSHC services offer Vacation Care on these days — but you need to book separately. Add them to your calendar in January for the whole year.

Sanity-saving tip: Block out your work calendar for the school holiday dates in January. Mark each week as "Vacation Care", "Family leave", or "Mixed". Then book accordingly. The cost of doing this 30-minute exercise in January is roughly zero. The cost of skipping it shows up in October.

Quick activity ideas for the days you're at home

If you've taken leave or you're working from home with a child around, here are five Perth-specific ideas that don't require much planning:

  • Beach + library combo — morning at the local beach (Coogee, Hillarys, Rockingham), afternoon at the library for the air-conditioned wind-down. Most Perth libraries run free holiday storytime sessions.
  • Free council holiday programmes — most City of Armadale, City of Rockingham and City of Swan locations run free activity days during the holidays. Check the council websites in the first week of term.
  • Kings Park nature walks — Lotterywest Family Area has shaded play space, walking trails, and (usually) school holiday discovery programmes.
  • Indoor backup plan — for the inevitable rainy week. Scitech, WA Museum (Boola Bardip), AQWA, Perth Zoo. All have one-day passes and good wet-weather options.
  • Friend-host swap — Tuesday at theirs, Thursday at yours. Two parents get half their week back; the children get to play with a friend. Win-win.

Common parent questions

Q: How early can I book Vacation Care?

A: Most Perth OSHC services open bookings 4-6 weeks before each break. Some open earlier for permanent families. The summer break is the most competitive — book as soon as the booking window opens.

Q: Is CCS the same for Vacation Care as during the term?

A: Yes — Vacation Care is subsidised at the same rate as your regular OSHC, calculated against the OSHC hourly rate cap ($9.93 for 2025-26). Your subsidy percentage depends on family income and activity hours.

Q: What if my child's age sits between primary and high school?

A: Most Vacation Care services accept children up to 12 years old. From 13, options shift to teen holiday programmes (council, YMCA, sports clubs) — usually shorter days and self-directed activities.

Q: Can I book individual days, or do I have to commit to a full week?

A: Most centres allow day-by-day booking, subject to availability. Excursion days often have a minimum participant count and may close earlier than other days.

Q: What's the actual cost per day?

A: It varies by centre and the type of programme that day (incursion vs excursion vs on-site). Roughly, expect $90-$140 per day before CCS. After CCS, eligible families often pay $20-$50 per day depending on subsidy rate. Ask your centre for an exact quote for your family.
