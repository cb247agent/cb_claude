Long Day Care vs OSHC: a Perth parent's plain-English guide

Updated June 2026 · The difference between LDC and OSHC, when to use each one, and how to pick what actually fits your family's week.

If you've ever tried to compare childcare options for your Perth family and walked away more confused than when you started, you're in good company. The terms — Long Day Care, OSHC, Vacation Care, Family Day Care — sound official, but no one really explains what they mean in plain language.

This guide is for working parents trying to figure out which type of care actually fits their week. We'll go through what LDC and OSHC are, who they're designed for, how the fees and Child Care Subsidy work for each, and how to decide between them — without the industry jargon.

What is Long Day Care (LDC)?

Long Day Care is exactly what it sounds like: childcare for the full working day. It's designed for children from around six weeks old up to school age (typically until they start Kindy or Pre-Primary at age 4-5). A standard LDC session runs from early morning to late afternoon — most Perth centres open between 6:30 and 7:00 AM and close around 6:00 PM.

Inside the centre, children are grouped into rooms by age — usually Babies (0-2), Toddlers (2-3), and Kindy (3-5). Each room has qualified early-childhood educators following an approved learning framework. Days include structured learning, free play, outdoor time, two main meals plus snacks, and a rest period for younger children.

LDC is the option most working parents picture when they think "childcare" — a single location their child attends multiple days per week, with the same educators, friends, and routine. At our LDC centres in Midvale, Seville Grove and Waikiki, most families enrol 2-5 days per week depending on their work pattern.

What is OSHC (Outside School Hours Care)?

OSHC is care for school-age children — typically 5-12 years old — during the hours when school isn't running. There are three flavours:

  • Before School Care (BSC) — usually 6:30 or 7:00 AM until school starts. Children get breakfast, a settled morning, and are walked or driven to school.
  • After School Care (ASC) — from school pick-up until 6:00 PM. Includes afternoon tea, homework time, structured activities, and free play.
  • Vacation Care (VAC) — full-day care during school holidays and pupil-free days. Excursions, incursions, and themed weeks are typical.

OSHC is built around the school day, not around the centre. Most OSHC services operate at or near a primary school, so the morning hand-off and afternoon pick-up are short. In Perth, we run OSHC at five locations — Armadale, Midvale, Rockingham, Seville Grove and Waikiki — each linked to specific primary schools in the area.

The short version: LDC is for children under school age, full days. OSHC is for school-age children, before-and-after-school plus holidays. If you have one child in pre-Kindy and one in Year 2, you'll likely need both.

How do the fees and CCS compare?

Both LDC and OSHC are approved childcare types under the Child Care Subsidy — meaning eligible families get a percentage of fees paid directly to the centre by Services Australia. But the underlying fee structure and CCS cap are different.

Daily session length

LDC charges for a full-day session — typically 10-11 hours of available care, whether or not you use all the hours. OSHC charges per session: a Before School session is around 2-2.5 hours, After School is around 3-3.5 hours, Vacation Care is a full day.

The CCS hourly cap

For the 2025-26 financial year, the CCS hourly rate cap is:

Care typeCCS hourly rate cap
Long Day Care$14.29 per hour
Outside School Hours Care$9.93 per hour
Vacation Care (under OSHC)$9.93 per hour

The cap matters because CCS is calculated as a percentage of either the fee you're charged or the cap, whichever is lower. Because OSHC sessions are short and the per-hour fee tends to sit close to the cap, families often see CCS cover a high proportion of OSHC fees. LDC fees can sit above the cap on some sessions, so the out-of-pocket gap is a little larger.

Your actual subsidy depends on your family income, your activity hours, and your child's age — we've written a separate plain-English CCS guide if you'd like the full picture.

How do I decide which one my family needs?

The decision usually comes down to the age of your child and the shape of your work week. Three questions to ask:

  1. How old is your child? Under school age → LDC. School age → OSHC. If you have children in both groups, you'll be juggling both.
  2. What hours do you work? If you need long days covered (e.g. 8:00-5:30), LDC for younger children and BSC + ASC for older children. If you only need before- or after-school cover, OSHC on its own is enough.
  3. What does the school holiday plan look like? School breaks happen 4 times a year. Vacation Care (under OSHC) is the standard fix — but it books out fast in Perth, so it's worth securing places early.

A typical Perth working-parent week might combine: 3 days LDC for a 2-year-old at one centre, plus Before + After School Care for a 7-year-old at another centre near their school. CCS applies to both, calculated separately for each child.

A note on transitions: Many parents move their child from LDC into OSHC the year they start school. If you're thinking about an OSHC service for next year, get on the waitlist 6-9 months in advance — Term 1 places fill from October onwards.

Common parent questions

Q: Can my 4-year-old in Kindy use OSHC?

A: Yes, if your child is attending Kindy at a school (not at a long day care centre), they can attend OSHC for Before and After School Care. Vacation Care is also available. Some centres allow Kindy children to attend OSHC; others limit to Pre-Primary and up — check with the specific OSHC service.

Q: Can a centre offer both LDC and OSHC?

A: Yes — many do. At My World Childcare, our Midvale and Seville Grove centres run both LDC and OSHC under one roof, which can simplify drop-off if you have a Toddler-aged and a school-aged child. Armadale and Rockingham are OSHC-only; Waikiki is LDC-only.

Q: Is OSHC cheaper than LDC?

A: Per session, yes — because OSHC sessions are shorter. Per hour, the headline rates are comparable, but the CCS hourly cap is lower for OSHC ($9.93 vs $14.29 for LDC). Your actual cost depends on your income, activity, and the centre's fee. Use a CCS calculator or ask your chosen centre for a personalised quote.

Q: What happens if I need care for a non-school day mid-term?

A: Pupil-free days are covered under Vacation Care at most OSHC services — book in advance, as numbers are capped. For unexpected closures or sick days, your regular OSHC session doesn't carry over, so check the centre's policy on absences.

Q: Do I need to commit to fixed days?

A: For LDC, yes — most centres enrol you on specific days per week (Mon/Wed/Fri, for example). For OSHC, you can usually book casual sessions on top of permanent days, subject to availability. Casual rates and CCS apply the same way.
