What is CCS and how do I apply? A Perth parent's plain-English guide

Updated June 2026 · A no-jargon walkthrough of the Child Care Subsidy for working parents in Western Australia.

If you've ever looked at a childcare invoice and wondered, "wait, what am I actually paying?" — you're not alone. The Child Care Subsidy (CCS) is the federal government's contribution to early-learning costs, but the rules around it can read like tax law. So here's the plain-English version.

We've written this for Perth families in particular. The CCS is national, but the way it shows up on your invoice depends on whether you're in Long Day Care (LDC), Outside School Hours Care (OSHC), or another arrangement — and there are a few WA-specific quirks worth knowing.

What is CCS, in one paragraph?

CCS is a government payment that reduces what you pay for approved childcare. It's not a cheque to you — it goes directly to your childcare provider, who applies it as a discount on your weekly fees. The amount depends on three things: your family's combined income, how much "approved activity" you do (work, study, volunteering), and what type of care you use.

The short version: Most working families on combined incomes under $533,280 are entitled to some level of CCS. The lower your income, the higher the percentage covered. The amount you actually receive also depends on hours you work or study.

Who's eligible?

To get CCS, you (and your partner, if you have one) need to meet four conditions:

  1. You're responsible for the child's care for at least 2 nights per fortnight — i.e., the child lives with you.
  2. The child is under 13 and not yet at secondary school (with limited exceptions for older children with disabilities).
  3. The child is fully immunised, on a recognised vaccination catch-up plan, or has a registered exemption.
  4. You use an approved childcare service — which means a centre with an "approved provider" number (every centre in WA that's open to government funding has one).

You also need to be an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or hold a specific visa. There are short-term exceptions if you've just arrived — Services Australia has a full list.

How much will I actually get?

Two factors determine the percentage of childcare costs CCS covers: your family income and your activity test result.

The taper rate is steep at the top end and gentle at the bottom — meaning most middle-income Perth families still get a meaningful subsidy. A family on $120,000 combined, for example, typically receives around 80% CCS.

2. The activity test

How many hours of subsidised care you can claim depends on how much "approved activity" you do per fortnight. Approved activity includes:

  • Paid work (including unpaid leave of up to 6 months, parental leave, or annual leave)
  • Self-employment
  • Study or training
  • Volunteer work
  • Looking for work (limited time)

If you're on combined income under $83,280 and have low activity, you may still be eligible for 36 hours of subsidised care per fortnight under the Child Care Safety Net.

How do I actually apply?

The application is done entirely through your myGov account, linked to Centrelink. Here's the step-by-step:

  1. Sign in to myGov at my.gov.au and link your Centrelink account if you haven't already.
  2. Start a Child Care Subsidy claim under the Centrelink menu. You'll need both partners' tax file numbers (TFN), payslips, and ID documents.
  3. Estimate your family income for the financial year. Be conservative — overestimating slightly avoids a debt at tax time.
  4. Confirm your activity hours. Each partner submits their own activity test details.
  5. Link your child's enrolment with the childcare centre once you've chosen one. The centre's "Provider ID" is on their website or in their welcome pack.

The form takes about 30-40 minutes if you have your documents ready. Centrelink usually processes the claim within 7-14 days. CCS then starts being applied to your invoices from the date you become eligible — not from the date you applied — so there's no rush to do it the day before care starts.

Tip: If you're switching centres, you don't need to re-apply for CCS. You just update your "Provider ID" with Services Australia and add the new centre's CRN.

What about Out of School Hours Care (OSHC)?

OSHC — Before School Care, After School Care, and Vacation Care — uses the same CCS framework as Long Day Care. The percentage covered and your hourly cap are calculated the same way.

A quirk worth knowing: hourly rate caps differ by care type. For 2025-26 the cap for OSHC is $9.93 per hour, while LDC is $14.29. This means CCS covers a higher proportion of a typical OSHC session because the rate is closer to what providers actually charge.

What happens if my situation changes?

The system was designed to flex. If your income changes (you take parental leave, get a pay rise, change jobs), update your estimate in myGov as soon as you know. CCS will recalculate from the next fortnight.

If your activity hours drop (you reduce work days, finish a course), your subsidised hours cap will drop too. You can update activity through myGov in about 5 minutes.

At the end of the financial year, Services Australia reconciles your actual income against your CCS estimates. If you under-estimated, you'll get a top-up; if you over-estimated, you may owe a small amount. Most families end up within a few hundred dollars either way — the system is forgiving.

Common parent questions

Q: Does CCS cover holidays when my child isn't attending?

A: Yes — up to 42 "absence days" per child per financial year (more for documented illness or family emergency). After that, you pay full fee for any further absences.

Q: Is there extra help for families in difficult situations?

A: Yes. The Additional Child Care Subsidy (ACCS) supports grandparents who are primary carers, transitioning-to-work families, families with temporary financial hardship, and children at risk of harm. These have separate application processes via Centrelink.

Q: How does CCS work if I have more than one child?

A: Each child has their own CCS rate, and from July 2022 the second-and-subsequent-children get a 30% higher CCS rate (capped at 95%). It's a meaningful saving for Perth families with two or more children in childcare.

Q: Can I claim CCS for a nanny or in-home carer?

A: Only for In Home Care, which is a specific approved service type — not for a private nanny arrangement. In Home Care is limited (about 3,200 places nationally) and only for families with complex needs that approved centres can't meet.

Q: My partner doesn't work — can we still get CCS?

A: If neither partner is doing approved activity, you may still qualify for the Child Care Safety Net (36 hours per fortnight) if your combined income is under $83,280. Above that threshold, both partners generally need to be doing some approved activity for CCS to apply.
