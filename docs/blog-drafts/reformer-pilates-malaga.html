Reformer Pilates vs mat Pilates — what's the difference

Mat Pilates uses bodyweight only. Reformer Pilates uses a spring-resistance machine — the reformer — to add load and support at the same time. This makes it useful for rehabilitation, progressive challenge for experienced practitioners, and people who want structured resistance training without heavy lifting.

The reformer controls range of motion and allows for more targeted muscle activation than mat work. It's also lower impact on joints. Some people find this suits them during recovery phases — always follow advice from your physio or doctor before returning to exercise after an injury.
